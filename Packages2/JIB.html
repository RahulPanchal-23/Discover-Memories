Jibhi - Tirthan Valley

Located in the Kullu district of Himachal Pradesh, Tirthan Valley derives its name from the Tirthan river, which winds its way through it. The pristine river originates from the icy cold glacial springs of Hanskund, a snow-capped peak in the verdant Great Himalayan National Park (GHNP). Tirthan is an offbeat destination that has something for everyone. This quiet valley is ideal for trekking, fishing, wildlife watching, and discovering under-explored hill villages. There’s also the option of doing absolutely nothing as you relax among the pine trees in the valley.

Short Tentative Itinerary

0th Day: Delhi to Jibhi
1st Day: Tirthan Arrival | Trek to chhoie waterfall
2nd Day: Jalori Pass, Sirolsar Lake Trek
3rd Day: River Rafting in Kullu and Departure from Jibhi

Tentative Itinerary


DAY 1: Tirthan Arrival | Trek to chhoie Waterfall


Leave Delhi at 7PM, Pick up from Kashmiri Gate/Majnu ka Tila (Day 0). Reach Tirthan by morning, Check-in to hotel and freshen up. Later Trek to Chhoie Waterfall. Come back to hotel by evening, enjoy a warm bonfire with good music and mouth-watering snacks Dinner and Overnight stay at the hotel.


DAY 2 : Jalori Pass & Trek to Serolsar Lake


Wake up early in the morning and after breakfast, drive to Jalori Pass. Hike to 360° Himalayan view point an enjoy the stunning landscape of snow-clad Himalayas. Trek further to the beautiful Serolsar Lake surrounded by lush forest cover and pay homage at Budhi Nagin Temple. Come back to the hotel by evening, enjoy a warm bonfire with good music. Dinner and overnight stay at the hotel.


DAY 3 : Jibhi Waterfall | Departure


Wake up to a peaceful mountain morning. Post breakfast, Check out from the hotel, and head towards Jibhi Waterfall In the afternoon, depart back to Delhi. Reach Delhi the next morning with Beautiful trip memories.
